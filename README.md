# Phishing Email Detection & Awareness System

## Project Overview

This project was completed as part of the Future Interns Cyber Security Internship Program.

The objective of this project was to analyze phishing email samples, identify phishing indicators, classify email threats based on risk levels, and develop security awareness guidelines to help users recognize and avoid phishing attacks.

This task focused on practical phishing detection, threat analysis, risk assessment, and security awareness, which are essential skills for Security Analysts and SOC Analysts.

---

## Objectives

- Analyze phishing email samples
- Identify phishing indicators and social engineering techniques
- Classify email threats based on risk level
- Document findings in a professional security report
- Develop phishing prevention and awareness guidelines
- Improve understanding of email-based cyber attacks

##  Phishing Indicators Identified

### 1. Generic Greeting
The email used a generic greeting ("Dear User") instead of addressing the recipient by their actual name.

### 2. Urgent Language
The message created a sense of urgency by warning that the account would be locked within 24 hours.

### 3. Suspicious URL
The provided URL appeared suspicious and did not belong to an official or trusted organization.

### 4. Request for Sensitive Information
The email attempted to convince users to verify account details, a common phishing tactic used to steal credentials.

### 5. Fear-Based Social Engineering
Threats of account suspension were used to manipulate users into taking immediate action without verification.

---

##  Risk Classification

**Risk Level:** High

**Classification:** Phishing Email

### Reason

The email contains multiple phishing characteristics, including:

- Suspicious URL
- Generic greeting
- Urgent language
- Social engineering tactics
- Credential harvesting attempts

These indicators significantly increase the likelihood of credential theft, unauthorized account access, and identity compromise.

---

## Prevention Guidelines

- Verify sender email addresses carefully.
- Inspect URLs before clicking links.
- Never share passwords or OTPs through email.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Report suspicious emails immediately.
- Avoid downloading attachments from unknown senders.
- Confirm requests through official communication channels.

---

## ✅ Do's and Don'ts

### Do's

- Verify sender information.
- Check URLs carefully.
- Enable MFA.
- Use strong passwords.
- Report suspicious emails.

### Don'ts

- Do not click unknown links.
- Do not share passwords.
- Do not share OTPs.
- Do not download suspicious attachments.
- Do not trust urgent requests without verification.
